  Saint Petersburg (, tr.: Sankt-Peterburg, ) is a city and a federal subject of Russia located on the Neva River at the east end of the Gulf of Finland on the Baltic Sea. St.&nbsp;Petersburg's informal name, Piter (), is based on how Peter the Great was called by foreigners. The city's other names were Petrograd (, 1914–1924) and Leningrad (, 1924–1991).Governor of Sankt Petersburg: [ ] Founded by Tsar Peter the Great on May 27, 1703, it was the capital of the Russian Empire for more than two hundred years (1713-1728, 1732-1918).
