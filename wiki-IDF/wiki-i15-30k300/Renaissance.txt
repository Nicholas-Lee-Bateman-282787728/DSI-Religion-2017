   The Renaissance (from French Renaissance, meaning "rebirth"; Italian: Rinascimento, from re- "again" and nascere "be born"Renaissance, Online Etymology Dictionary) was a cultural movement that spanned roughly the 14th through the 17th century, beginning in Italy in the late Middle Ages and later spreading to the rest of Europe. It encompassed a revival of learning based on classical sources, the development of linear perspective in painting, and educational reform.   The Renaissance saw developments in most intellectual pursuits, but is perhaps best known for its artistic aspect and the contributions of such polymaths as Leonardo da Vinci and Michelangelo, who have inspired the term "Renaissance men".BBC Science & Nature, Leonardo da Vinci (Retrieved on May 12 2007)BBC History, Michelangelo (Retrieved on May 12 2007) There is a widely-held belief, by no means unchallenged, that the Renaissance began in Tuscany in the fourteenth century.P. Burke, The European Renaissance: Centre and Peripheries (Blackwell, Oxford 1998) Various theories have been proposed to account for the distinctiveness of this period and to explain its origins and characteristics, focusing on an assortment of factors, including the social and civic peculiarities of Florence at this time including its political structure and the patronage of its dominant family, the Medici.
