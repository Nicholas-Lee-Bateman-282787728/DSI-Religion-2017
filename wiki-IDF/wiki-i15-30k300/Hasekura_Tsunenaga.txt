He led a diplomatic mission to the Pope in Rome, via New Spain (Mexico) and various ports of call in Europe, between 1613 and 1620 (called the Keichō Embassy, ), after which he returned to Japan. He was the first Japanese official ambassador to the Americas and Rome (and arguably Europe),In Europe, Hasekura was preceded by the mission of Mancio Ito, under the guidance of Alessandro Valignano in 1582–1590, which is called the Tenshō Embassy in Japanese (). This embassy was dispatched under the initiative of three Western Japan daimyos Omura Sumitada, Otomo Sorin and Arima Harunobu, and it is unclear whether it was also sponsored by the Shogun. and initiated the first recorded instance of Franco-Japanese relations.
